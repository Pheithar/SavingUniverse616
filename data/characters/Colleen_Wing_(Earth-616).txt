===Early Life===
Colleen Wing was the daughter of [[Lee Wing (Earth-616)|Professor Lee Wing]], a professor of Oriental studies at New York's [[Columbia University]]. Her mother, [[Azumi Ozawa (Earth-616)|Azumi Ozawa]], was apparently killed by an unknown assailant while Colleen was still a child.{{r|Shadowland: Daughters of the Shadow Vol 1 3}} Colleen grew up in the mountains of northern Honshu in Japan, where she was raised by her grandfather, [[Kenji Ozawa (Earth-616)|Kenji Ozawa]]. Kenji's other children died in [[World War II]]. Kenji trained his only grandchild in the arts of the samurai, which his own ancestors had been in the time of the daimyo.{{r|Power Man and Iron Fist Vol 1 70}} Kenji had also been a member of Japan's secret service.{{r|Deadly Hands of Kung Fu Vol 1 32}} Kinji was murdered by Hong Kong crime lord [[Emil Vachon]]. Colleen made her home in New York City where her father lived. Caught in the midst of a gun battle on Manhattan's West Side, she was saved from being killed by police officer [[Mercedes Knight (Earth-616)|Misty Knight]], who soon became her best friend.{{Citation}}

===Nightwing Restorations, Ltd===
After Misty lost her arm, Colleen encouraged Knight to rise above her depression and return to an active role in life. After Knight's right arm was replaced with a bionic steel arm, Colleen and Misty formed a partnership as private investigators and called their firm Nightwing Restorations, Ltd.{{r|Deadly Hands of Kung Fu Vol 1 32}} Due to Wing's samurai training and both partners' expertise in the martial arts, they were dubbed "the Daughters of the Dragon".{{r|Deadly Hands of Kung Fu #29}} The two specialized in missing persons cases, and teaming up with Iron Fist on many missions.{{r|Deadly Hands of Kung Fu #32}}{{r|Deadly Hands of Kung Fu #33}} They battled the [[Wrecking Crew (Earth-616)|Wrecking Crew]]{{r|Iron Fist #11}} and [[Frederick Myers (Earth-616)|Boomerang]].{{r|Iron Fist #13}}

===Iron Fist===
Professor Wing learned from a monk that [[Daniel Rand (Earth-616)|Iron Fist]] was arriving from K'un-Lun. He calculated when Iron Fist would come to New York and sent Colleen to meet him.{{r|Marvel Premiere #19}} Professor Wing and Colleen befriended Iron Fist, and Colleen acted as his ally in many of his exploits.

Colleen was kidnapped by [[Khan (Earth-616)|Master Khan]] who used [[David Angar (Earth-616)|Angar the Screamer]] to brainwash her into killing Iron Fist. When he arrived to free her, she attacked him, but Iron Fist managed to free her from Angar's influence by bridging their minds. She then defeated Angar in a duel, severely injuring him with her sword.{{r|Iron Fist #5|7}}

She helped Iron Fist clear his name when he was framed for the supposed murder of [[William Hao (Earth-616)|Bill Hao]].{{r|Iron Fist #10}}

Colleen was romantically involved with actor [[Robert Diamond (Earth-616)|Robert Diamond]], one of the [[Sons of the Tiger (Earth-616)|Sons of the Tiger]].{{Citation}}

===Ricadonna===
[[File:Daughters of the Dragon Vol 1 6 Textless.jpg|thumb|Daughters of the Dragon]]
She continued working with Misty, reforming the Daughters of the Dragon and becoming bail bondsman, lending bail money to apprehended criminals until their cases worked their way through the criminal justice system. When one of the criminals missed their court date, they would hunt them down and bring them to justice. They also hired [[Otis Johnson II (Earth-616)|Otis Johnson]] to work in the office. They investigated [[Celia Ricadonna (Earth-616)|Celia Ricadonna]] a wealthy New York publishing magnate and crime lord suspected of involvement in the deaths of some of their clients.{{r|Daughters of the Dragon #1}} Criminals [[Jack Croft (Earth-616)|Freezer Burn]], [[Buchanan Mitty (Earth-616)|Humbug]], [[Jeff Hagees (Earth-616)|8-Ball]], and [[David Cannon (Earth-616)|Whirlwind]] were hired to steal a microchip containing a computer virus capable of taking down the global economy.{{r|Daughters of the Dragon #2}} She attempted to kill all the criminals involved in the heist.{{r|Daughters of the Dragon #3}} When Colleen confronted Misty, she sliced off her bionic arm. Tony Stark, however, was able to provide an upgraded version.{{r|Daughters of the Dragon #4}} Teaming up with Iron Fist and one of their client [[Buchanan Mitty (Earth-616)|Humbug]], they found the auction for the microchip, turned Ricadonna into the authorities.{{r|Daughters of the Dragon #5|6}}

===Civil War/Initiative===
During the [[Civil War]]; Coleen and Misty were contacted by [[Anthony Stark (Earth-616)|Iron Man]], [[Reed Richards (Earth-616)|Reed Richards]], and [[Peter Parker (Earth-616)|Spider-Man]] to reform [[Heroes for Hire (Knight & Wing) (Earth-616)|Heroes for Hire]] in order to track down superhumans refusing to comply with the [[Superhuman Registration Act]]. Initially hesitant, Colleen accepted, but only with the conditions that they would hunt criminals not registering, get Stark level resources, and she would get to pick the team. the pair eventually created a team including [[Shang-Chi (Earth-616)|Shang-Chi]], [[Buchanan Mitty (Earth-616)|Humbug]], [[Orka (Earth-616)|Orka]], [[Felicia Hardy (Earth-616)|Black Cat]], [[Paladin (Earth-616)|Paladin]], and a new [[Maria Vasquez (Earth-616)|Tarantula]].{{r|Heroes for Hire Vol 2 #1|2}}
