| History                 = The Friends of Humanity was a group started by [[Graydon Creed (Earth-616)|Graydon Creed]], a man infamous for his bigotry against [[Homo superior|mutants]]. He and his [[homo sapiens|human]] disciples have caused damage on many occasions whenever there is a possibility to harm mutant rights, and they are agitating to force lawmakers to reinstate the [[Mutant Registration Act]]. When Graydon, a powerless baseline human, learned of his heritage, that he was the son of two mutants who had abandoned him as an inconvenience, he grew resentful of all mutants, and that resentment colored his outlook for the rest of his life. He funnelled his hatred into forming the Friends of Humanity.{{citation}}

[[Steven Lang (Earth-616)|Steven Lang]] was found by the FoH at the [[Eden Park Nursing Home]] and sent a member to recover him. He was able to release him from custody since the desk nurse was also a member.{{r|Uncanny X-Men Vol 1 291}} The FoH's influence seeped across the US and the [[X-Men (Earth-616)|X-Men]] became aware of them after they kidnapped [[Russell Collins (Earth-616)|Rusty Collins]] and [[Sally Blevins (Earth-616)|Skids]] from the [[Vault (Prison)|Vault]] using the [[Electromagnetic Scrambling Pulse]]. This led to news reporter [[Patricia Tilby (Earth-616)|Trish Tilby]] later reported on them.{{r|X-Force Vol 1 24}} 

The FoH came to protest the parole release of the mutant hero [[Vance Astrovik (Earth-616)|Vance Astrovik]] after he accidently killed his abusive father [[Arnold Astrovik (Earth-616)|Arnold]]. Creed appeared on many TV shows to protest. Vance was not able to hold back his rage and attacked the protestors with his telepathic powers.{{r|New Warriors Vol 1 43}} 

In [[New York City]], the FoH gathered around a police precinct which was holding a murder suspect who just happened to be a mutant. As they protest outside, [[Lucia Callasantos (Earth-616)|Lucia Callasantos]] was interrogated by [[Jose Hidalgo (Earth-616)|Jose Hidalo]] inside. [[X-Force (Earth-616)|X-Force]] decided to come and help the young mutant They tried to move her for the trial but the guards turned out to be bought off by the FoH. They tried to follow them and was anble rescue both Hidalgo and Thornn from an ambush.{{r|X-Force Vol 1 40}}

[[James Proudstar (Earth-616)|Warpath]] and [[Gloria Mu√±oz (Earth-616)|Risque]] witnessed the FoH attacking a man for being razed a mutant. The man told the group that he is not a mutant and they threatened to beat him up still. They saved the man from the group and they ran off, but the man was not grateful as he blamed the mutants for the problem in the first place.{{r|X-Force Vol 1 56}}

On a night out, [[Kyle Gibney (Earth-616)|Wild Child]] and [[Shard Bishop (Earth-1191)|Shard]] head to underground nightclub to listen to a rock band. They tried to join a mosh pit, but they was confronted by members of FoH, but they were no match for the mutant duo, knocking the thugs out before leaving.{{r|X-Factor Vol 1 119}}

Creed's growing influence allowed him to campaign for the [[President of the United States of America|Presidency of America]].{{r|X-Factor #130}} During this time, some members of the FoH attacked [[Trevor Chase (Earth-616)|Trevor Chase]], the grandson of [[Irene Adler (Earth-616)|Destiny]]. They beat him so badly he ended up in a coma in a hospital. [[Raven Darkholme (Earth-616)|Mystique]] kept an eye on the family of her former love and learned if the attack. Mystique decided to assassinate Creed hoping to put and end to his rain of terror.{{r|X-Factor Vol 1 127}} At a presidential rally, an assassin shot and killed him.{{r|X-Factor #130}} It was revealed to be future version of Mystique who fired the shot killing Creed.{{r|X-Men Forever #2}}

[[Donovan Zane (Earth-616)|Donovan Zane]] led an [[Empire State University]] faction of the anti-mutant group the FoH.{{r|Spider-Man Vol 1 82}} The group bullied and harassed students in their search for mutants.{{r|Spider-Man Vol 1 77|78}} [[Gwendolyne Stacy (Earth-616)|Gwen Stacy]]'s cousin [[Paul Stacy (Earth-616)|Paul Stacy]] joined the FoH and attempted to intimidate academic rival and mutant [[Robin Vega (Earth-616)|Robin Vega]] into leaving the college, but he was eventually driven off by [[Peter Parker (Earth-616)|Peter Parker]].{{r|Spider-Man Vol 1 82}} After his apartment was stormed by the FoH, Paul helped the other tenants escape, and rushed back into the building to save a child named [[Character Index/A#Angela|Angela]].{{r|Spider-Man Vol 1 83}}

[[Christopher Bradley (Earth-616)|Christopher Bradley]] was attacked by the FoH when his parents were trying to seek treatment for their mutant son when he was infected by the [[Legacy Virus]]. [[Christoph Nord (Earth-616)|Maverick]], another Legacy sufferer, helped Chris and his family relocate to [[Florida (State)|Florida]] with new identities.{{r|X-Men Unlimited Vol 1 15}} The FoH eventually found him and planned to terminate Chris but Maverick was tracking the young mutant. He came to the rescue and forced the FoH to flee.{{r|Maverick Vol 2 4}}

[[Sebastion Gilberti (Earth-616)|Sebastion "Bastion" Gilberti]] emerged through a portal the merged being of [[Master Mold (Earth-616)|Master Mold]] and [[Nimrod (Earth-811)|Nimrod]] thanks to the [[Siege Perilous]]. He was taken in by [[Rose Gilberti (Earth-616)|Rose Gilberti]] and learned about the growing mutant problem so he joined the FoH to fight the threat. He began to remember his true origin and began to use his cybernetic abilities to destroy mutants.{{r|Machine Man / Bastion Annual Vol 1 '98}}

A group of [[Purifiers (Earth-616)|Purifiers]] later dug up Creed's corpse and took his body to their base where it was re-animated by Bastion using the [[Techno-Organic Virus]]. He later let the [[Sapien League (Earth-616)|Sapien League]] attack a FoH rally much to sadness killing the human there creating a distraction.{{r|X-Force Vol 3 12}} Later Creed died again in the battle against the X-Men.{{r|X-Force Vol 3 28}}

The FoH sent a squadron of Ninja assassins to kill the members [[X-Factor Investigations (Earth-616)|X-Factor Investigations]]. When the arrived in full Ninja garb they was met by [[Pip Gofern (Earth-616)|Pip the Troll]] on reception. He took them out with ease using a portable [[Glossary:Wormhole|Wormhole]] generator. {{r|X-Factor Vol 1 246}}

After the public wedding of [[Jean-Paul Beaubier (Earth-616)|Northstar]] and [[Kyle Jinadu (Earth-616)|Kyle Jinadu]], the FoH began targeting human family members of mutants and attempted to assassinate Kyle. The [[X-Men (Earth-616)|X-Men]] and Northstar were able to thwart the attack, but it left Kyle shaken.{{r|Astonishing X-Men Annual Vol 1 1}}

[[Steven Rogers (Earth-616)|Captain America]] went under cover at a political protest in which the Friends of Humanity clashed with the Red Skulls, the [[Watchdogs (Earth-616)|Watchdogs]] and the Sons of Selene. He was force to intervene when the groups started fighting.{{r|Captain America Vol 9 27}}

In the wake of [[Knull (Earth-616)|Knull]]'s [[King in Black (Event)|invasion]], the Friends of Humanity reconvened under the leadership of [[Peter Krane (Earth-616)|Senator Peter Krane]], whose xenophobic anti-extraterrestrial message resonated with those traumatized by the symbiote attacks. However, the group was infiltrated by the [[Carnage IV (Symbiote) (Earth-616)|Carnage symbiote]], with [[Cletus Kasady (Earth-616)|Cletus Kasady]] plotting to use it as a vehicle to make a comeback.{{r|Extreme Carnage Alpha Vol 1 1}}
